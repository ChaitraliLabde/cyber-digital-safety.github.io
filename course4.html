🌐 Safe Internet Usage

Navigate the internet safely and securely

📚 Learning Objectives

  • Browse the internet safely
  • Identify secure websites
  • Understand browser security
  • Learn about safe downloading

📖 Course Content

🧭 1. Safe Browsing Practices

🔹 What Is Safe Browsing?

Safe browsing means using the internet responsibly — avoiding harmful sites, protecting your data, and staying alert while online.

🔹 Key Safe Browsing Tips:
  • Use trusted search engines (like Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo)
  • Avoid suspicious or illegal websites (pirated content, adult, gambling)
  • Keep your browser updated to patch security vulnerabilities
  • Install an ad-blocker or browser security extension
  • Log out after using banking, email, or social media on shared devices
  • Use Private/Incognito mode on public computers
🔹 Why It Matters
  • Unsecured browsing can expose your device to malware
  • Phishing attacks and identity theft are common on unsafe sites

🔒 2. Recognizing Secure Websites (HTTPS, SSL, etc.)

🔹 What Is HTTPS?

HTTPS encrypts the data between your browser and the website.

  • Look for a lock 🔒 icon in the address bar
  • Ensure the address starts with https://
🔹 SSL/TLS Certificates

Certificates verify site identity and enable encryption. Without SSL, information you send (passwords, cards) can be intercepted.

🔹 Always Check:
  • ✅ Lock symbol in the browser bar
  • ✅ "https://" instead of "http://"
  • ✅ Correct spelling (beware of lookalikes like faceb00k.com)

Example: ✅ https://www.onlinesafety.gov.in   ❌ http://www.onlinesafetygov.in

⚠️ 3. Avoiding Dangerous Downloads and Links

🔹 Types of Dangerous Content:
  • Fake apps or software from unofficial websites
  • Email attachments from unknown senders
  • Pop-up ads offering free gifts or prizes
  • “Too good to be true” links
🔹 Safety Tips:
  • Download only from official sources (Play Store, App Store, verified sites)
  • Hover over links to preview where they lead
  • Avoid torrent or cracked software sites
  • Use reliable antivirus that scans downloads automatically
  • Never install extensions or apps you do not trust

Example: If an email says “Your parcel is waiting — click here!”, do not click. Verify the sender or check the official website.

📶 4. Public Wi‑Fi Risks and Safe Usage

🔹 What Are Public Wi‑Fi Networks?

Public Wi‑Fi in cafés, airports, libraries is often unsecured, so others on the network may intercept your data.

🔹 Common Risks:
  • Data theft and activity monitoring
  • Fake Wi‑Fi networks imitating real ones
  • Session hijacking of logged-in accounts
🔹 Safe Practices:
  • Avoid logging into bank or personal accounts
  • Use a VPN to encrypt your traffic
  • Turn off auto-connect to open networks
  • Forget the network after use
  • Enable your device’s firewall

🕶 5. Using VPNs for Privacy

🔹 What Is a VPN?

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) encrypts your connection and hides your IP address, making your browsing private and secure.

🔹 Benefits of Using a VPN:
  • Keeps your location and data private
  • Protects your connection on public Wi‑Fi
  • Allows secure access to regional content
  • Prevents ISP tracking and targeted advertising
🔹 How to Use VPNs Safely:
  • Choose trusted providers (ProtonVPN, NordVPN, ExpressVPN)
  • Avoid free VPNs that may sell your data
  • Turn VPN on before connecting to public networks
  • Disconnect when done to save battery/data

🧠 Key Takeaways

💻 Safe Browsing

Visit trusted, secure sites and avoid illegal ones

🔒 HTTPS & SSL

Always check for “https://” and the lock icon before sharing info

📦 Downloads & Links

Avoid unknown links and untrusted downloads

📶 Public Wi‑Fi

Be cautious; use VPNs to protect your data

🕶 VPNs

Encrypt your activity for maximum privacy

🧠 Real-World Example

Imagine you’re at a café using free Wi‑Fi to check your email. Without a VPN, a hacker nearby can intercept your login info. With a VPN and secure HTTPS websites, your connection is encrypted — your data stays safe.
