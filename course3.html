üîê 2. Importance of Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

üî∏ What Is 2FA?

Two-Factor Authentication adds an extra layer of protection by requiring two verification steps:

  1. Something you know ‚Äî your password
  2. Something you have ‚Äî OTP, email code, or authentication app
üî∏ Common 2FA Methods:
  • OTP via SMS or email
  • Authenticator apps (like Google Authenticator, Authy)
  • Biometric verification (fingerprint, face ID)
üî∏ Why It's Important:
  • Prevents hackers from logging in even if they know your password
  • Protects sensitive accounts like banking, email, and social media
  • Adds strong defense against phishing and credential theft

üß∞ 3. Managing Passwords Safely (Password Managers)

üî∏ What Is a Password Manager?

A password manager is a secure tool that stores all your passwords in an encrypted vault, accessible with one master password.

üî∏ Benefits:
  • Remembers and auto-fills your passwords
  • Generates strong, random passwords
  • Reduces password fatigue (no need to memorize dozens)
  • Syncs securely across devices
üî∏ Examples of Trusted Password Managers:
  • Bitwarden
  • 1Password
  • LastPass
  • Dashlane
  • Google Password Manager
üî∏ Safety Tips:
  • Use a strong master password
  • Enable 2FA on your password manager
  • Never share your vault or export passwords carelessly

üö® 4. Recognizing Suspicious Login Attempts

üî∏ Common Warning Signs:
  • You receive login alerts from unknown devices or locations
  • You notice password reset emails you didn't request
  • You're logged out suddenly from multiple devices
  • You find unusual activity (posts, messages, transactions)
üî∏ What to Do:
  • Immediately change your password
  • Enable 2FA if not already done
  • Review your login history or account activity
  • Report suspicious access to the platform (e.g., Google, Instagram, etc.)

üßØ 5. What To Do If Your Account Is Hacked

üîπ Step-by-Step Recovery:
  1. Stay Calm ‚Äî Don't panic.
  2. Reset your password immediately using "Forgot Password."
  3. Use a recovery email/phone number to regain access.
  4. Enable 2FA once you recover the account.
  5. Check linked apps and devices ‚Äî remove any unknown access.
  6. Scan your device for malware or keyloggers.
  7. Inform contacts ‚Äî warn them about any suspicious messages sent from your account.
  8. Report to authorities if financial or personal data was stolen (via cybercrime.gov.in).
