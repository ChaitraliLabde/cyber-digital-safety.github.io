🧾 Complete Cybercrime Reporting Guide

Step-by-step instructions for reporting cybercrimes safely and effectively

🧾 1. How to Report a Cybercrime (Step-by-Step Guide)

If you experience or witness any form of cybercrime — such as online fraud, social media harassment, identity theft, or hacking — follow these simple steps to report it safely and effectively:

🪜 Step 1: Collect Evidence

  • Take screenshots of chat messages, emails, or posts.
  • Save transaction details, phone numbers, or email IDs involved.
  • Note the date, time, and platform where the incident occurred.

🪜 Step 2: Report Online via Official Portal

You can register your complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (Government of India):

🔗 Website: https://cybercrime.gov.in

Steps on the Portal:

  1. Visit the website and click on "File a Complaint."
  2. Choose the category – "Women/Child Related Crime" or "Other Cybercrime."
  3. Read and accept the terms and conditions.
  4. Fill in your personal details (name, contact, email).
  5. Upload supporting evidence (screenshots, documents, etc.).
  6. Submit your complaint and note the complaint ID for future tracking.

🪜 Step 3: Report to Local Police or Cyber Cell

If the issue is serious or urgent:

  • Visit your nearest police station or cybercrime cell.
  • Provide printed copies of your complaint and evidence.
  • You can file an FIR (First Information Report) — it's your right under Indian law.

🪜 Step 4: Follow Up

  • Keep your complaint ID safe to track updates on the portal.
  • Respond promptly to any follow-up communication from authorities.

☎️ 2. Official Helpline Numbers (India)

If you need immediate help or advice, reach out to these verified helplines:

Purpose Helpline Number Availability
National Cybercrime Helpline 📞 1930 24x7
Cybercrime Reporting Portal 🌐 cybercrime.gov.in Online 24x7
Women Helpline 📞 1091 24x7
Child Helpline 📞 1098 24x7
National Helpline for Online Financial Fraud 📞 1930 Immediate Blocking of Fraudulent Transactions

💡 Tip: If you suspect financial fraud, call 1930 immediately — banks can block stolen funds within the "Golden Hour" (first few hours after the fraud).

❓ 4. FAQs: What To Do If You're a Victim

Q1. I've been scammed online — what's the first thing I should do?

👉 Immediately report the incident at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.
Change your passwords, inform your bank (if financial data is involved), and preserve all evidence.

Q2. Someone is harassing me on social media.

👉 Block and report the profile on the platform.
If harassment continues, lodge a complaint on cybercrime.gov.in under "Women/Child Related Crimes."

Q3. I accidentally shared my OTP or card details. What now?

👉 Contact your bank helpline right away and request to block your account/card.
Then file a complaint on 1930 and cybercrime.gov.in.

Q4. Can I file a complaint anonymously?

👉 Yes, you can file a complaint without disclosing your identity for crimes involving women or children.

Q5. I'm not sure whether what happened to me is a cybercrime.

👉 Visit the "Types of Cybercrimes" section on the portal. You can also call 1930 for guidance.
