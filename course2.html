🛡 Online Threats and Cyber Crimes

Understanding different types of cyber threats and criminal activities

📚 Learning Objectives

  • Identify various types of cyber threats
  • Understand common cybercrime patterns
  • Recognize phishing and social engineering
  • Learn about malware and ransomware

📖 Course Content

⚠️ 1. Introduction

The internet is a powerful tool — but it also opens the door to cyber threats and online crimes that can cause serious harm. Cybercriminals use technology to steal information, money, or identities, and sometimes to harass or mislead people.

Learning about these threats is the first step to staying safe online.

🧩 2. Phishing and Email Scams

💬 What is Phishing?

Phishing is a technique used by cybercriminals to trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. It often comes through emails, text messages, or fake websites that look legitimate.

📧 Common Examples:
  • An email pretending to be from your bank asking to "verify your account."
  • A message from a fake e-commerce site about a "refund" or "gift voucher."
  • A SMS claiming you've "won a lottery" and asking for personal details.
🛡️ How to Protect Yourself:
  • Do not click on links or attachments from unknown senders
  • Check the email address and URL carefully — small spelling mistakes are common in fake sites
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts
  • If unsure, contact the organization directly via their official website

💡 Tip: Legitimate companies will never ask for your password, OTP, or PIN via email or message.

🦠 3. Malware, Viruses, and Ransomware

💻 What is Malware?

Malware stands for "malicious software." It includes programs designed to damage, steal, or control your device or data.

⚙️ Types of Malware:

Virus: Attaches to files and spreads between computers.

Trojan Horse: Pretends to be a safe file but gives hackers control of your system.

Spyware: Secretly monitors your online activity and steals personal data.

Ransomware: Locks your files and demands payment (ransom) to unlock them.

🧰 How Malware Spreads:
  • Downloading files from untrusted websites
  • Clicking infected links in emails or ads
  • Installing pirated software
  • Using infected USB drives
🛡️ Protection Tips:
  • Use trusted antivirus software and update it regularly
  • Avoid downloading from unknown sources
  • Keep your operating system and apps updated
  • Regularly back up your files to avoid data loss

🚫 Never pay ransom to cybercriminals — instead, contact law enforcement or cybersecurity experts.

🕵️ 4. Identity Theft and Data Breaches

💳 What is Identity Theft?

When someone uses your personal information — like your name, Aadhaar number, or bank details — without permission, it's called identity theft.

📂 What is a Data Breach?

A data breach happens when hackers gain unauthorized access to sensitive data (e.g., customer databases, login credentials, etc.).

⚠️ Examples:
  • A hacker stealing your card details from an online shopping site
  • Someone using your identity to open fake accounts or loans
  • A leaked database exposing millions of users' passwords
🛡️ How to Stay Safe:
  • Use unique passwords for every account
  • Don't share personal information on social media
  • Check your bank and credit card statements regularly
  • Use secure (HTTPS) websites for online transactions
  • Enable account alerts to monitor suspicious activity

💡 If your identity is stolen, report it immediately at cybercrime.gov.in and to your bank.

💰 5. Online Fraud and Financial Scams

🪤 What are Online Frauds?

These are crimes where people are tricked into giving money or financial details through fake websites, messages, or calls.

💣 Common Types of Scams:
  • Fake Online Shopping Sites: Offer cheap deals but never deliver products
  • Lottery/Prize Scams: Claim you've "won money" but ask for a "processing fee"
  • Job Scams: Offer fake jobs and ask for "registration" or "training" fees
  • Investment Scams: Promise "guaranteed returns" or "double your money"
  • UPI/OTP Scams: Trick you into sharing OTPs or scanning QR codes to steal funds
🛡️ How to Avoid Them:
  • Shop only on reputed websites (look for HTTPS & reviews)
  • Never share your OTP, CVV, or PIN
  • Be skeptical of "too good to be true" offers
  • Call 1930 (National Helpline) immediately if scammed

⚠️ Remember: Real companies don't ask for money upfront to give you a job, loan, or prize.

🧠 6. Social Engineering Attacks

🕴️ What is Social Engineering?

It's when criminals manipulate people psychologically into revealing information or performing actions that compromise security.

💬 Examples:
  • A fake IT support person calling to "fix your computer"
  • A scammer posing as your boss asking for an urgent money transfer
  • Fraudsters pretending to be bank officials verifying your details
🧰 How to Defend Yourself:
  • Verify the person's identity before sharing information
  • Don't rush — scammers often create fake urgency
  • Be cautious about what you share on social media
  • Remember: Genuine officials never ask for confidential information

💡 The weakest link in cybersecurity is often human trust — stay alert!

💬 7. Cyberbullying and Online Harassment

😔 What Is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the act of intimidating, threatening, or humiliating someone through digital platforms like social media, chat apps, or games.

💢 Examples:
  • Spreading rumors or private information online
  • Posting insulting comments or memes
  • Sending abusive messages repeatedly
  • Impersonating someone to harm their reputation
🛡️ How to Handle It:
  • Don't respond or retaliate
  • Take screenshots as evidence
  • Block and report the bully
  • Inform parents, teachers, or authorities
  • File a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in

🧡 Cyber kindness matters — use technology to uplift, not harm.

📰 8. Fake News and Misinformation

🧠 What is Fake News?

Fake news refers to false or misleading information spread online to deceive or manipulate people.

📢 Forms of Misinformation:
  • Altered images or videos (deepfakes)
  • Fake health or political news
  • Misleading headlines that twist facts
  • Viral social media posts without reliable sources
🔍 How to Identify Fake News:
  • Check the source and author
  • Verify using trusted fact-checking sites (like PIB Fact Check, Alt News)
  • Look for grammar errors or exaggerated claims
  • Don't forward anything before confirming its authenticity

⚠️ Sharing fake news can cause panic, hate, or even legal trouble — Think Before You Share!

🧠 Key Takeaways

⚠️ Threat Awareness

Cyber threats are real and constantly evolving.

🛡️ Best Defense

Awareness and caution are your best defense.

✅ Verify First

Always verify before you click, share, or trust.

📞 Report Issues

Report suspicious or criminal activity to cybercrime.gov.in.

Stay updated with the latest safety tips and tools.
